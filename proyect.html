Art Proyect

TFM: Environment

×

Project Details

For my final master’s project, I developed a video game environment concept focused on visual storytelling and atmosphere. The project features two contrasting biomes: a vast desert landscape and a frozen Arctic setting. Each environment was designed to convey a distinct mood and narrative, using light, color, and composition to immerse the player in the world. The two locations are connected through magical portals, allowing players to travel between extreme climates and explore how each environment reflects different story elements. My process included concept sketches, layout exploration, and moodboards, followed by detailed illustrations developed in Photoshop and 3D support using Blender for composition and lighting references. This project allowed me to combine my passion for environment storytelling, worldbuilding, and gameplay integration, while pushing my technical and artistic skills in the development of immersive digital spaces.

In addition to my final master's project, I developed several other works focused on both character and environment design, using a combination of 3D sculpting, photobashing, and digital painting: Miles Morales Character Illustration: I created a 3D sculpt of Miles in ZBrush to define anatomy, pose, and expression. Project Details

n addition to my concept art work, I have developed several 3D character and environment projects that allowed me to explore a full production workflow from sculpt to render. These projects were created using the following pipeline: ZBrush for high-resolution sculpting Maya for retopology and clean mesh preparation Mixamo for automatic rigging and posing Substance Painter for texture painting and materials Marvelous Designer for realistic cloth simulation Blender for environment modeling and scene layout Marmoset Toolbag for final rendering and presentation Some of the completed character projects include: An elven archer, focused on graceful anatomy and layered costuming A stylized rabbit, with an expressive pose and cartoon-inspired textures Sara, a modern character concept based on a bass guitarist, blending urban style and attitude A fantasy king, wearing ornate robes and accessories designed to convey power and elegance Each project helped me refine my understanding of the 3D pipeline, from anatomical sculpting and clothing to posing, texturing, and presentation. I particularly enjoy combining realism and stylization, bringing characters and environments to life in a visually engaging and technically polished way.

Programation Proyect

TFG: Scaperoom in Unity

×

Project Details

As part of my undergraduate final project, I developed a narrative-driven detective game using Unity. The goal of the game is to solve a mini murder case by exploring different suspect locations, gathering clues, and analyzing evidence to identify the killer. The player moves between interactive environments, such as rooms in each suspect’s home, where they can investigate objects, read documents, and uncover hidden items. The game features simple inventory logic, dialogue sequences, and progressive clue unlocking that leads to the final deduction. This project allowed me to combine game design, storytelling, and programming, focusing on player agency and logical thinking. It was built entirely in Unity, with C# scripts handling object interactions, dialogue triggers, and state changes across the investigation. This experience strengthened my understanding of game logic, scene management, and event systems, while also highlighting how narrative and mechanics can work together to deliver an engaging gameplay experience.
